Structured across three trimesters, students will progress from foundational robotics principles and EV3 building to advanced sensor-based programming and robot challenges, culminating in 3D printing projects that address real-world challenges aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.Course Objectives:Ą Introduction to Robotics: Develop a foundational understanding of robotics and its role in solving real-world SDG challenges.Ą LEGO Mindstorms EV3 Proficiency: Master EV3 components, sensors, motors, and block-based programming.Ą SDG Integration: Connect robotics projects to the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.Ą Advanced Programming: Apply loops, conditionals, and sensor integration to complete complex robot missions.Ą 3D Printing with Tinkercad: Explore digital fabrication by designing 3D models applicable to robotics, engineering, and SDG problem-solving.Ą Teamwork and Collaboration: Work effectively in teams to design, build, and present robotics and 3D design projects.Ą Creativity and Innovation: Propose and prototype creative solutions using both robotics and 3D printing.Ą Presentation and Advocacy: Document and present SDG-focused projects, developing communication skills.STUDENT OUTCOMESStatement1231. LEARNING COMPETENCIES Đ ROBOTICS 7TRIMESTER 1 COMPETENCIESA. Foundations of Robotics and SocietyĄ A.1 Define robotics and describe how robots are different from ordinary machines.Ą A.2 Identify types of robots and explain how they are used in daily life and industries.Ą A.3 Describe how sensors help robots understand and react to their surroundings.Ą A.4 Explain how robotics can help solve real-world problems (e.g., pollution, health, education).Ą A.5 Relate robotics to the UN SDGs by identifying how robots can support goals like clean energy, climate action, and quality education.B. LEGO Mindstorms EV3 BasicsĄ B.1 Name the main parts of the LEGO EV3 set: Intelligent Brick, motors, and sensors.Ą B.2 Use the EV3 Classroom software to make the robot move and respond to simple commands.Ą B.3 Follow safety rules while assembling LEGO robot models.Ą B.4 Download and run a program on the EV3 Brick to perform a task.Ą B.5 Fix basic issues when the robot does not behave as expected.C. Building Strong and Useful RobotsĄ C.1 Build a robot that is stable and can perform a task without falling apart.Ą C.2 Improve the robot's strength, speed, or grip by using correct parts and design.Ą C.3 Attach tools to help the robot do specific jobs (e.g., push, grab, or lift items).Ą C.4 Explore how gears can make robots move faster or stronger.Ą C.5 Test and improve robot designs based on how well they work.G. Robotics in Action: SDG ProjectsĄ G.1 Design, build, and program robots to complete SDG-themed challenges.Ą G.2 Collaborate with a team to solve problems using robotics.Ą G.4 Use problem-solving steps (plan, build, test, improve) during robot activities.Ą G.5 Apply robotics in real-life scenarios through class SDG projects.TRIMESTER 2 COMPETENCIESD. Programming with Logic and ControlĄ D.1 Use loops to make the robot repeat actions.Ą D.2 Use if-then rules in programming so the robot makes decisions based on sensors.Ą D.3 Arrange blocks in the correct order to create more complex robot actions.Ą D.4 Use wait blocks and variables to control time and responses in a program.Ą D.5 Find and fix errors in a robot's code to make it perform correctly.E. Using Sensors for Smart BehaviorĄ E.1 Program the robot to react to colors using the color sensor.Ą E.2 Make the robot stop or avoid things using the ultrasonic sensor.Ą E.3 Combine sensors (like touch and color) in one program for more complex actions.Ą E.4 Understand that sensors may not always be perfect; learn how to test them.Ą E.5 Create programs where sensors tell the robot when to move, stop, or turn.F. Moving on Its Own: Autonomous NavigationĄ F.1 Program the robot to follow a line using the color or light sensor.Ą F.2 Create programs that help the robot avoid obstacles using loops and sensor input.Ą F.3 Test, observe, and improve how the robot moves through a path or maze.G (continued). Robot ChallengesĄ G.3 Participate in robotics challenges and reflect on what worked and what needs improvement.TRIMESTER 3 COMPETENCIESG (continued). Advanced Robot ChallengesĄ G.1 Design and program robots to complete navigation, delivery, and sorting challenges.Ą G.2 Collaborate with a team to solve complex robotics problems under time pressure.Ą G.3 Reflect on challenge results and suggest improvements in design or coding.Ą G.4 Use plan-build-test-improve steps during all robot games and activities.I. Introduction to 3D PrintingĄ I.1 Define 3D printing and explain how it works (additive, layer-by-layer manufacturing).Ą I.2 Identify common uses of 3D printing in robotics, medicine, engineering, architecture, and daily life.Ą I.3 Relate 3D printing to the UN SDGs, explaining how it can contribute to sustainable solutions.Ą I.4 Differentiate between types of 3D printing filaments (PLA, ABS) and their appropriate uses.J. Tinkercad Design SkillsĄ J.1 Create an account and navigate the Tinkercad workspace (workplane, shape library, view controls, ruler).Ą J.2 Place, resize, move, and rotate basic 3D shapes on the workplane with precision.Ą J.3 Use the grouping feature to combine multiple shapes into a unified object.Ą J.4 Apply hole shapes to subtract material from solids to create openings, slots, or cutouts.Ą J.5 Use the alignment and mirror tools to position and duplicate shapes accurately.Ą J.6 Export a completed design as an STL file ready for 3D printing.K. 3D Design ProjectsĄ K.1 Design a simple everyday object (e.g., keychain, nameplate) as a starter Tinkercad project.Ą K.2 Design a custom robot part or accessory (e.g., sensor mount, claw, custom wheel) for EV3 or Arduino.Ą K.3 Apply engineering design principles (strength, form, function) to improve a 3D model through iteration.Ą K.4 Identify a real-world problem linked to an SDG and propose a 3D-printed solution.Ą K.5 Design and complete a Tinkercad model that addresses the chosen SDG problem.Ą K.6 Present and explain the 3D design, its purpose, and its SDG connection to the class.COURSE REQUIREMENTSWritten WorksĄ Term Examination Đ Once per trimester;
