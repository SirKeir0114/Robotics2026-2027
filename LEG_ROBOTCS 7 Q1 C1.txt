Group Brainstorm (5 mins)o Divide class into small groups (3Ŵ4 students).o Ask: ÒWhere do you see robots being used in real life?Óo Groups list as many examples as they can (home, school, hospitals, factories, space, etc.).2. Robot Type Cards (10 mins)o Provide sets of Robot Type Cards with pictures and short descriptions (e.g., service robots, industrial robots, medical robots, military robots, space exploration robots, entertainment robots).o Provide Scenario Cards describing real-life tasks (e.g., Òassembling cars,Ó Òdelivering packages,Ó Òperforming surgery,Ó Òexploring MarsÓ).3. Match and Explain (10 mins)o Groups match each Scenario Card to the correct Robot Type Card.o For each match, they write a short explanation:* ÒThis task uses a ______ robot because it is designed to ______.Ó4. Class Sharing (5 mins)o Each group shares one or two of their matches with the class.o Teacher clarifies and reinforces correct understanding.	transferPerformance standard: The learner is able to explain what robotics is, differentiate robots from machines, and describe how sensors help robots solve real-life problems.Activity Instructions1. Scenario Brief (5 mins)o Teacher presents this challenge:* ÒA disaster has struck a city. Your team must design a rescue robot that can navigate rubble, detect trapped survivors, and avoid obstacles using sensors.Ó2. Review of Sensor Types (5 mins)o Brief class review of common robot sensors and what they do:* Ultrasonic/infrared sensors Ŵ detect distance and avoid obstacles* Touch sensors Ŵ detect contact or collision* Light sensors Ŵ detect brightness/darkness* Sound sensors Ŵ detect sound signals or voices* Temperature/gas sensors Ŵ detect heat or dangerous gases3. Robot Design Task (15Ŵ20 mins)o In small groups, students:* Choose at least 3 different sensors for their rescue robot.* Draw their robot design on paper.* Label the sensors and describe how each one helps the robot react to its surroundings (e.g., ÒThe ultrasonic sensor will help it avoid crashing into walls.Ó).4. Presentation and Reflection (5Ŵ10 mins)o Groups present their robot design to the class.o Peers give feedback on how well the sensors match the mission needs.	Progress And INTERVENTION trackerReflection:Progress And INTERVENTION trackerTeaching Techniques [Check the weekly TLAC Techniques]?
